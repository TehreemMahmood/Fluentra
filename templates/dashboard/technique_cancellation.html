Cancellation Technique

A powerful post-stuttering strategy that helps you take control after a moment of dysfluency occurs.

The Four Steps

  1. Complete the Word: Finish saying the stuttered word completely - don't stop midway
  2. Pause Deliberately: Stop and take a brief, intentional pause (2-3 seconds)
  3. Analyze the Moment: Think about what happened - where was the tension? What did you feel?
  4. Retry with Control: Say the word again using a fluency technique (light contact, easy onset)
  5. Continue Speaking: Move forward with your message confidently

Benefits for Fluency

  • Takes Back Control: You choose to address the stutter rather than rushing past it
  • Builds Awareness: Develops understanding of your personal stuttering patterns
  • Reduces Fear: Confronting stuttering directly lessens anxiety about it
  • Improves Confidence: Shows yourself you can handle stuttering moments
  • Teaches New Patterns: Reinforces fluent speech motor patterns

How Cancellation Reduces Stuttering

Breaks Avoidance Patterns

Instead of rushing through or avoiding difficult words, cancellation teaches you to face them directly, breaking the cycle of fear and avoidance.

Develops Self-Monitoring

The pause and analysis step trains you to become aware of what happens during stuttering, which is essential for making changes.

Reinforces Fluent Patterns

By saying the word again with a fluency technique, you create a new, successful motor memory for that word and situation.

Clinical Evidence

Developed by Dr. Charles Van Riper, cancellation is a cornerstone of stuttering modification therapy. Research shows it effectively reduces both the frequency and severity of stuttering by building awareness and voluntary control. Studies indicate that consistent use of cancellation can lead to a 30-50% reduction in stuttering severity over time.

Example in Practice

Situation: You're trying to say "I want to go to the b-b-b-beach"

Step 1: Finish the word: "...beach" (even if stuttered)
Step 2: Pause for 2-3 seconds
Step 3: Think: "I felt tension in my lips, I was pressing too hard"
Step 4: Say again with light contact: "beach" (smoothly)
Step 5: Continue: "...this weekend"

Tips for Effective Practice

1
Don't Rush

The pause is essential. Take your time to truly analyze the moment.

2
Be Honest with Yourself

Really feel what happened - where was the tension? What triggered it?

3
Practice in Safe Settings First

Start with family or friends before using in high-pressure situations.

4
Celebrate the Attempt

Every cancellation is a success, regardless of how the retry goes.
