Fluency Shaping

Prolonged Speech

Slow down and stretch your sounds to achieve smoother, more controlled speech and reduce stuttering moments.

How to Practice

  1. Stretch Your Vowels: Extend each vowel sound for 1-2 seconds ("Iiiii waaant")
  2. Slow Transitions: Move smoothly between sounds without abrupt stops
  3. Continuous Airflow: Keep air flowing throughout words and phrases
  4. Connect Words: Link the end of one word to the beginning of the next
  5. Gradually Speed Up: Slowly increase your rate as fluency improves

Benefits for Fluency

  • Reduces Time Pressure: Slowing down eliminates the rush that triggers stuttering
  • Improves Coordination: Gives your brain time to coordinate breathing, voice, and articulation
  • Eliminates Blocks: Continuous phonation prevents hard blocks from forming
  • Builds Muscle Memory: Establishes smooth speech as the new default pattern
  • Increases Confidence: Predictable fluency reduces speaking anxiety

How Prolonged Speech Reduces Stuttering

Reduces Muscle Tension

Slower speech allows your speech muscles to stay relaxed. High tension in the jaw, lips, and tongue is a primary cause of stuttering.

Maintains Continuous Phonation

By keeping your voice running continuously, you prevent the vocal cord closures that cause blocks and repetitions.

Allows Processing Time

Slowed speech gives your brain extra time to plan upcoming sounds, reducing the timing errors that contribute to stuttering.

Clinical Evidence

Prolonged speech is one of the most researched fluency shaping techniques. The Camperdown Program and other evidence-based treatments show 70-90% reduction in stuttering frequency using this approach. Long-term studies indicate that skills gained through prolonged speech practice are maintained years after treatment.

Practice Sentences

Practice these sentences using prolonged speech. Start very slow, then gradually increase speed while maintaining smoothness.

"Hello, my name is..."

Stretch: Heee-lloo, myy naaame iis...

"I would like to order..."

Stretch: Iii woould liiike tooo orrder...

"Can you help me with..."

Stretch: Caaan yoou heelp meee wiith...

"I have a question about..."

Stretch: Iii haave aa queestion aabout...

Tips for Effective Practice

1
Start Slower Than You Think

Begin at 30-50% of your normal speed. It should feel uncomfortably slow at first.

2
Focus on Vowels

Vowels are the "glue" of speech. Stretching them creates the continuous flow.

3
Practice Daily

15-20 minutes of daily practice is more effective than occasional longer sessions.

4
Transfer Gradually

Move from reading → monologue → conversation → phone calls → presentations.
