Articulation Technique

Light Articulatory Contacts

Reduce physical tension in speech by using gentle, relaxed movements when producing consonant sounds.

How to Practice

  1. Identify Target Sounds: Focus on plosive consonants like P, B, T, D, K, and G
  2. Reduce Pressure: Touch your articulators (lips, tongue) together very lightly
  3. Gentle Release: Release the sound with minimal force, letting it "slide" out
  4. Practice Words: Start with single words, then progress to phrases
  5. Monitor Tension: Keep checking that your jaw, lips, and tongue remain relaxed

Benefits for Fluency

  • Prevents Blocks: Light contacts reduce the chance of getting "stuck" on sounds
  • Reduces Physical Effort: Speaking becomes less tiring and more natural
  • Smoother Transitions: Easier movement between sounds in connected speech
  • Less Struggle: Minimizes the physical struggle behavior during stuttering
  • Increased Confidence: Speaking feels easier, building communication confidence

How Light Articulatory Contacts Reduce Stuttering

Reduced Muscular Tension

Excessive pressure at articulatory contact points is a major trigger for blocks. Light contacts prevent the "locking" sensation that leads to stuttering moments.

Maintained Airflow

When articulators touch lightly, air can still flow through more easily, preventing the complete airflow stoppage that characterizes blocking.

Easier Sound Initiation

Light contacts make it easier to initiate and release sounds, reducing the effort needed to start speaking and transition between syllables.

Clinical Evidence

Research in fluency shaping therapy shows that light articulatory contacts are one of the most effective techniques for reducing blocks on plosive sounds. Studies indicate that people who stutter often use 2-3 times more pressure than fluent speakers when producing consonants. Training in light contacts normalizes this pressure and significantly reduces stuttering frequency.

Practice Words by Sound

P & B Sounds

Peter, paper, butter, baby, bubble, purple

T & D Sounds

Turtle, water, dinner, daddy, today, ladder

K & G Sounds

Kitten, cookie, gentle, giggle, coming, garden

Tips for Effective Practice

1
Use a Mirror

Watch your lips and jaw to ensure you're making gentle, relaxed movements.

2
Exaggerate at First

Start with extremely light contacts, then gradually find a natural balance.

3
Combine with Breathing

Take a relaxed breath before practicing to reduce overall tension.

4
Transfer to Conversation

Once comfortable, practice light contacts during real conversations.
